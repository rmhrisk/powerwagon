## Earlier vehicle

An earlier Desert Power Wagon, commissioned by the owner — built by Tisdale Coachworks and
finished by Desert Power Wagons. A different vehicle, and built differently: the earlier truck is
a ground-up build, its bare-metal steel body and chassis from Tisdale at the original Power
Wagon's width, with a rebuilt engine from a donor Ram 2500. This truck instead retains the chassis
and drivetrain of a 2022 Ram 2500 Power Wagon, its coachwork widened to fit that chassis.

### Car and Driver — feature

**6 July 2024** · https://www.caranddriver.com/features/a61211477/2024-desert-power-wagon/
· © Hearst Autos

`2024-07-06-car-and-driver-article-2024-desert-power-wagon.pdf` — 5 pages, captured from the
published page.

Ezra Dyer's feature on a ground-up Desert Power Wagon: chassis and body by Tisdale Coachworks of
Winslow, Indiana; four-link suspension with Eibach Race Off-Road springs and 3.0-inch
internal-bypass King dampers; Dana 60 front and Dana 80 rear axles; Wilwood disc brakes; rebuilt
6.7L Cummins rated at 385 hp and 930 lb-ft through a six-speed automatic. Also describes a second
build path from a $425,000 base price — the custom bodywork mounted on a new heavy-duty Ram
chassis, retaining the modern Ram interior, with the bed floor raised to clear the frame rails
and suspension.

### Robb Report — first drive

**3 August 2024** · https://robbreport.com/motors/cars/first-drive-desert-power-wagon-1235798532/
· © Robb Report Media, LLC

`2024-08-03-robb-report-article-desert-power-wagon-first-drive.pdf` — 23 pages, captured from the
published page.

Howard Walker's first drive of a midnight-blue four-door. Records the Desert Power Wagon as
starting at $385,000, with roughly 2,400 hours of labour over a nine-to-twelve month build, and
names Tisdale Coachworks of Indiana as the fabricator of the body and chassis. Engine options are
the 6.7L Cummins turbo-diesel, a supercharged 6.2L Hellcat V8, and the 6.4L HEMI V8.
