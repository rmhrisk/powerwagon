01

Born in War

When America went to war, Dodge stopped building for civilians and turned out the WC series of light military trucks. The three-quarter-ton weapons carrier earned a reputation as the truck that would go anywhere and never quit.

02

The Boys Wrote Home

Many of those soldiers were farm boys. They came home wanting the truck that had carried them through the war and wrote to Dodge asking where a civilian could buy one. Dodge answered.

03

The First 4×4 You Could Buy

In 1946 Dodge put the proven military running gear under a civilian cab and called it the Power Wagon. It was the first four-wheel-drive truck an ordinary buyer could drive off a dealer lot from a major manufacturer.

04

A Power Plant on Wheels

Dodge sold it as a truck, a tractor, and a portable power plant in one. With power take-off at the front, center, and rear, it could run a well rig, a saw, a generator, or a pump anywhere you could drive it.

05

It Wouldn't Retire

American sales ended in 1968, but the trucks lived on. Armies and farmers ran them for decades, from rural France to the Philippines, and Israel kept building its own version into the 1980s.

06

The Name Comes Back

Ram revived the Power Wagon in 2005 as the factory off-road 2500 and marked its 75th anniversary in 2021. The idea held from 1946: serious capability built in at the factory, not bolted on later.

Sold not as a truck, but as a power plant you could drive straight to the work.

DID YOU KNOW
  • The Philippine army ran its wartime Dodges into the 1980s, and Israel built its own into the same decade.
  • The crew cab began as coachbuilt trucks for rail and utility crews, before any factory offered one.
  • It was the first mass-produced 4×4 from a major automaker. Earlier four-wheel-drive trucks were military or aftermarket conversions.
